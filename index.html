Renaissance Students Are At Risk!

Mr. M's Digital Literacy eighth grade class designed a Social Media survey after studying the ways that social media are addictive and harmful, watching and discussing The Social Dilemma (Netflix) and creating an infographic about teen mental health and social media (Greenfield Recorder). The survey was shared with all crews, and a quarter of our students responded.

We are now sharing our results * as promised with this webpage about time online, social media and teen mental health.

In our survey and readings, we found that teens are more anxious, more depressed, and sometimes suicidal. This is not only because of the pandemic's affects on them and their families, but also because of how they used social media to cope with quarantine last year. The social media habits stayed with them, particularly 8th graders.

This is an ongoing problem for all of us. Here is a student analysis of some of the results; below are additional graphs.
